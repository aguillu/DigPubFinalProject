Broad Interests

I am committed to understanding how children go beyond the information they receive to learn about the world. This is integral in contributing meaningful, impactful knowledge on the systems that support early childhood learning. Thus far, my research experiences have sparked endless questions for me concerning childhood learning. What excites me about cognitive psychology research, at its core, is understanding how children learn from being active participants in the world around them.

Supervised Projects

Having worked in the Goldin-Meadow Lab at the University of Chicago, I have extensive experience in coding naturalistic parent-child interactions for higher order thinking talk (HOTT) I currently work in the Chatterlab under Dr. Marisa Casillas at the University of Chicago, where I am primarily involved in data annotation and experimental participant testing using eye-tracking methods.

Independent Projects

At the Harvard Graduate School of Education, I work in collaboration with Dr. Meredith Rowe and Dr. Ran Wei in designing and piloting a research project that investigated HOTT in parents and their children during narrative and pretense episodes. I have also crafted my honors thesis research project examining the ways in which children with pre- and parietal lesions use the shrug gesture in their spontaneous speech.
